[1] "Speaker Identification Based on a Matrix Quantization Method", Hsiao-Chuan Wang, Ming-Shih Chen, and Pei-Hwa Lin, 1993-01

 

In this paper,we can get better recognition rates if the testing digits selected. Hence,we can get better recognition rates if the testing digits are chosen carefully. The experiment has shown that MQ is a very promising method for speaker identification. The codebook size should be at least 128 to ensure the high accuracy when short utterance is used for testing.
 
[2] "Speaker Recognition Using Neural Networks and Conventional Classifier", Kevin R. Farrell, Richard J. Mammone, and Khaled T. Assaleh, 1994-01

 

An evaluation of various classifiers for text-independent speaker recognition is presented. In addition, a new classifier, modified neural tree network(MNTN), is examined for this application. The MNTN is compared with nearest neighbor classifiers, full-search, and tree-structured vector quantization(VQ) classifiers, multilayer perceptrons (MLP¡¦s), and decision trees.
 
[3] "Integrated Models of Signal and Background with Application to Speaker Identification In Noise", D. A. Reynolds, E. M. Hofstetter and R. C. Rose, 1994-04

 

This paper is concerned with the problem of robust parametric model estimation and classification in noisy acoustic environments. The techniques described here provide a mechanism for integrating parametric models of acoustic background with the signal model so that noise compensation is tightly coupled with signal model training and classification.
 
[4] "Experimental Evaluation of Features for Robust Speaker Identification", Douglas A.Reynolds, 1994-10

 

This correspondence presents an experimental evalution of different features and channel compensation techniques for robust speaker identification.
 
[5] "Microphone Arrays and Speaker Identification", Qiguang Lin, Ea-Ee Jan and James Flanagan, 1994-10

 

Hands-free operation of speech processing equipment is sometimes desired so that the user is unencumbered by hand-held or body-worn microphones. This paper explores the use of array microphones to capture speech under adverse acoustic conditions, and provide input to a system for automatic speaker identification. The paper also explores the influence of vector quantization techniques, codebook size, and order of cepstrum coefficients on the performance of the speaker identification system.
 
[6] "New LP-Derived Features for Speaker Identification", Khaled T. Assaleh and Richard J.Mammone,1994-10

 

A new set of features is introduced that has been found to improve the performance of automatic speaker identification systems. The new set of features is referred to as the adaptive component weighting(ACW) cepstral coefficients.
 
[7] "Robust Text-Independent Speaker Identification Using Gaussain Mixture Speaker Models", Douglas A. Reynolds and Richard C. Rose, 1995-01.

 

This paper has introduced and evaluated the use of Gaussian mixture speaker models for robust text-independent speaker identification. The Primary focus of this work was on a task domain for real applications, such as voice mail labeling and retrieval. The Gaussian mixture speaker model was specifically evaluated for identification tasks using short duration utterances from unconstrained conversational speech, possibly transmitted over noisy telephone channels.
 
[8] "A Comparative Study of Robust Linear Predictive Analysis Methods with Applications to Speaker Identification", Ravi P.Ramachandran,Mihailo S. Zilovic,and Richard J.Mammone, 1995-03

 

Both the WLAV and the IWLS approaches deemphasize the larger residual samples. Consequently,the pitch pulses in the LP residual are more apparent when using these two methods.
 
[9] "A Modified HME Architecture for Text-Dependent Speaker Identification", Ke Chen, Dahong Xie and Huisheng Chi, 1996-09

 

A modified hierarchical mixtures of experts (HME) architecture is presented for text-dependent speaker identification. A new gating network is introduced to the original HME architecture for the use of instantaneous and transitional spectral information in text-dependent speaker identification. The statistical model underlying the proposed architecture is presented and learning is treated as a maximum likelihood problem: in particular, an expectation-maximization (EM) algorithm is also proposed for adjusting the parameters of the proposed architecture.
 
[10] "HMM-based stressed speech modeling with application to improved synthesis and recognition of isolated speech under stress", Bou-Ghazale, S.E, Hansen, 1998-05

 

This paper proposed and implemented a novel modeling approach for speech parameter variations under stress using HMM¡¦s. effective stress modification algorithms can be formulated that successfully impart stress or emotion onto neutral speech for improved performance as judged by human listeners for speech synthesis, as well as improved robustness for speech recognition application.
 
[11] "Speaker Identification Based on the Use of Robust Cepstral Features Obtained from Pole-Zero Transfer Functions", Mihailo S. Zilovic, Ravi P. Ramachandran, and Richard J. Mammone, IEEE1998-05

 

The performance under mismatched training and testing conditions is a good measure of robustness. There is some variation in the relative robustness of the features for different conditions. However, the ACW, PFL1, and PFL2 cepstrum perform as well as or better than the LP cepstrum for all the test conditions.
 
[12] "Binary Quantization of Feature Vectors for Robust Text-Independent Speaker Identification", Zhong-Xuan Yuan, Bo-Ling Xu, and Chong-Zhi Yu, 1999-01

 

This paper has proposed an approach to quantize feature vectors: BQ. In order to demonstrate the effectiveness of BQ, the performance criterion of vector quantization, distortion (distance) measure, was employed.
 
[13] "A Comparison of Speaker Identification Results Using Features Based on Cepstrum and Fourier¡VBessel Expansion", K. Gopalan, Timothy R. Anderson, and Edward J. Cupples, 1999-05

 

This paper presents the results of speaker identification experiments using features obtained from the Fourier-Bessel expansion and the cepstral representation of speech frames.
 
[14] "A Discriminative Training Algorithm for VQ-Based Speaker Identification", Jialong He, Li Liu, and G¡Lunther Palm, 1999-05

 

A novel method, referred to as group vector quantization (GVQ), is proposed to train VQ codebooks for closed-set speaker identification. In GVQ training, speaker codebooks are optimized for vector groups rather than for individual vectors.
 
[15] "Robust Text-Independent Speaker Identification over Telephone Channels", Hema A. Murthy, Fran¡Mcoise Beaufays, Larry P. Heck, and Mitchel Weintraub, 1999-09

 

This paper addresses the issue of closed-set text-independent speaker identification from samples of speech recorded over the telephone. It focuses on the effects of acoustic mismatches between training and testing data, and concentrates on two approaches: extracting features that are robust against channel variations and transforming the speaker models to compensate for channel effects.
 
[16] "Modeling of the Glottal Flow Derivative Waveform with Application to Speaker Identification", Michael D. Plumpe, Thomas F. Quatieri, and Douglas A. Reynolds, 1999-09

 

An automatic technique for estimating and modeling the glottal flow derivative source waveform from speech, and applying the model parameters to speaker identification, is presented.
 
[17] "Speaker Identification for Security Systems Using Reinforcement-Trained pRAM Neural Network Architectures", Trevor G. Clarkson, Chris C. Christodoulou, Yelin Guan, Denise Gorse, David A. Romano-Critchley, and John G. Taylor, 2001-02

 

Four probabilistic pRAM neural network architectures are presented in order to explain how the different pRAM network architectures perform a classification and to show where the difficulties lie in separating different speakers using their TESPAR representations.
 
[18] "Capture Interspeaker Information With a Neural Network for Speaker Identification", Lan Wang, Ke Chen, and Huisheng Chi, 2002-03

 

This paper has proposed a novel method for the use of interspeaker information to improve performance of a model-based speaker identification system. A neural network is employed to capture the interspeaker information from the output space of those statistical models.
